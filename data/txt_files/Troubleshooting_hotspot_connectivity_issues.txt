QuickConnect Customer Support
Troubleshooting Hotspot Connectivity Issues
This document is designed to help QuickConnect customer service agents troubleshoot hotspot connectivity issues with customers. Follow the steps below to identify and solve common problems.
Step-by-Step Troubleshooting Guide

Check Device Compatibility

Ensure the customer’s device is compatible with the QuickConnect hotspot.
Verify that the device supports the appropriate frequency bands (2.4 GHz and/or 5 GHz).


Signal Strength

Ask the customer to move closer to the hotspot.
Check for physical obstructions (walls, furniture) that may be blocking the signal.


Network Settings

Ensure the hotspot is turned on and transmitting a signal.
Ask the customer to restart both the hotspot and their device.
Verify that the Wi-Fi network is not hidden.


Interference

Check if other electronic devices (e.g., microwaves, cordless phones) are causing interference.
Try changing the Wi-Fi channel on the hotspot.


Software Updates

Ensure the hotspot firmware is up to date.
Check if the customer’s device software/OS is up to date.


Data Limits

Verify if the customer has exceeded their data limit.


Reset Network Settings

Guide the customer to reset their network settings on both the hotspot and their device.



Common Issues and Resolutions



Issue
Possible Cause
Resolution




Cannot find the wireless network
Hotspot turned off, hidden network
Ensure the hotspot is on, check SSID broadcast settings


Weak signal
Physical obstructions, distance from hotspot
Move closer to the hotspot, remove obstructions


Intermittent connection
Interference from other devices
Change Wi-Fi channel, reduce interference sources


No internet access
Data limit reached, network outage
Check data usage, verify network status
