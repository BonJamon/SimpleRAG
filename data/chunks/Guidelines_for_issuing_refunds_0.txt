Guidelines for Issuing Refunds
As a QuickConnect customer service agent, you may be required to issue refunds to customers under certain circumstances. Following these guidelines will ensure that refunds are issued correctly and efficiently.
Eligibility Criteria
Refunds may be issued if any of the following conditions are met:

Service disruption for more than 24 hours
Incorrect billing
Unauthorized charges
Cancellation of service within the first 30 days

Steps to Process a Refund


Step
Description


1. Verify Eligibility
Confirm that the customer's request meets the eligibility criteria for a refund.


2. Gather Information
Collect necessary details including the customer’s account information, billing statements, and details of the issue.


3. Calculate Refund Amount
Determine the correct amount to be refunded based on the service issue or billing error.


4. Process Refund
Use the QuickConnect system to process the refund and ensure it is credited to the customer’s account or original payment method.


5. Notify Customer
Inform the customer about the refund process, the amount, and expected time for the refund to appear in their account.


6. Document the Case
Record all relevant information in the customer's case file for future reference.


Important Notes

Always maintain professionalism when handling refund requests.
Double-check all information before processing the refund to avoid errors.
If in doubt, consult with a supervisor to ensure the correct protocol is followed.
Provide the customer with a confirmation number and update them on the status of their refund if there are any delays.

Common Scenarios


Scenario
Action


Service Outage Compensation
Calculate the prorated amount for the duration of the outage and process the refund.


Incorrect Billing
Verify the billing error, correct the invoice, and issue the refund for the overcharged amount.


Unauthorized Charges
Investigate the unauthorized charge, ensure it is removed, and refund the customer's account.


Early Cancellation
Refund any applicable amounts based on the customer’s cancellation within the first 30 days of service.


Customer Communication
When communicating with customers regarding refunds, ensure the following:

Be clear and concise about the process and timeline.</
