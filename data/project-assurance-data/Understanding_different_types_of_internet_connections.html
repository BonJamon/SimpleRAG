Understanding Different Types of Internet Connections

QuickConnect offers a variety of internet connection options to suit different needs and budgets. As a customer service agent, it is essential to understand the different types of internet connections available so you can better assist our customers. Below, we provide an overview of the different types of internet connections, their features, benefits, and ideal use cases.

Types of Internet Connections

Connection Type Features Benefits Ideal Use Cases
DSL (Digital Subscriber Line)
  • Uses existing telephone lines
  • Speeds up to 100 Mbps
  • Widely available
  • Cost-effective
  • Reliable for basic internet usage
  • Light internet usage
  • Web browsing
  • Email
Cable
  • Uses coaxial cable network
  • Speeds up to 1 Gbps
  • Shared bandwidth with neighbors
  • Fast download speeds
  • Widely available in urban areas
  • Streaming services
  • Online gaming
  • Downloading large files
Fiber Optic
  • Uses glass or plastic fibers
  • Speeds up to 1 Gbps or higher
  • Reliable and consistent performance
  • Extremely fast speeds
  • Low latency
  • Heavy internet usage
  • Smart home devices
  • Multiple users and devices
Satellite
  • Uses satellites orbiting Earth
  • Speeds up to 100 Mbps
  • Available in remote areas
  • Accessibility in rural and remote areas
  • Areas without cable or fiber access
  • Basic internet needs
Fixed Wireless
  • Uses radio signals
  • Speeds up to 100 Mbps
