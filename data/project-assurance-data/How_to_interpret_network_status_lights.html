How to Interpret Network Status Lights

Overview

Network status lights on modems and routers can provide valuable information about the device's current status and connection health. This guide will help you understand and interpret these status lights to troubleshoot connectivity issues effectively.

Common Status Lights and Their Meanings

Light Color Status Description
Power Green On The device is powered on and functioning normally.
Power Red On There's a hardware issue or the device is malfunctioning.
Internet Green Blinking Data is being transmitted or received.
Internet Green Stable Connected to the internet successfully.
Internet Red Stable or Blinking Failed connection to the internet.
Wi-Fi Green Blinking Wireless data is being transmitted or received.
Wi-Fi Green Stable Wi-Fi is enabled and functioning correctly.
LAN (Ethernet) Green Blinking Data is being transmitted or received through a wired connection.
LAN (Ethernet) Green Stable Established connection through an Ethernet cable.

Troubleshooting Tips

  • Make sure all cables are securely connected.
  • Restart the modem or router by unplugging it, waiting 30 seconds, and plugging it back in.
  • Check your ISP's website or contact them directly to see if there is an outage in your area.
  • If the power light is red, try plugging the device into a different power outlet.
