How to Handle and Report Spam Complaints

Step-by-Step Guide:
  1. Listen to the Customer: Understand the issue by asking relevant questions to identify the nature of the spam complaint.
  2. Verify the Details: Confirm the customer's account information and note any specifics about the spam they are reporting (e.g., date and time received, sender details).
  3. Log the Complaint: Use the internal Complaint Management System (CMS) to record all details of the spam complaint. Ensure accuracy of information.
  4. Instruct the Customer: Provide guidelines to the customer on how to block spam messages and adjust their spam filters.
    • Advise the customer on how to forward spam texts to 7726 (SPAM).
    • Guide them through the process of changing email filters to reduce spam messages.
  5. Escalate if Needed: If the spam complaint is severe or recurring, escalate it to the technical team for further investigation.
  6. Follow Up: Ensure you or a team member follows up with the customer to confirm the issue has been resolved or to provide additional assistance.

FAQs:
  • Q: What should I do if a customer reports multiple spam messages?
    A: Log each complaint separately and advise the customer to use the spam reporting service by forwarding messages to 7726.
  • Q: Can the customer block spam numbers themselves?
    A: Yes, most devices allow users to block numbers. Provide them with instructions specific to their device.
