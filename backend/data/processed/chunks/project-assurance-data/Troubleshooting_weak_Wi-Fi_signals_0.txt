Troubleshooting Weak Wi-Fi Signals

Common Causes of Weak Wi-Fi Signals

Distance from Router: The further away a device is from the Wi-Fi router, the weaker the signal.
Physical Obstructions: Walls, floors, and furniture can block or degrade Wi-Fi signals.
Interference: Other electronic devices, such as microwaves and cordless phones, can interfere with Wi-Fi signals.
Router Placement: The placement of the Wi-Fi router can significantly affect the signal quality.
Outdated Equipment: Older routers and devices may not support the latest Wi-Fi standards and technologies.
Network Congestion: Too many devices connected to the network can reduce signal performance.
