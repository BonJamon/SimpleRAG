How to Explain VoIP Service Benefits
Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) is a technology that allows you to make voice calls using a broadband Internet connection instead of a regular (or analog) phone line. Key Benefits of VoIP Service

Cost Savings: VoIP services often reduce communication costs, particularly for international and long-distance calls.
Flexibility and Mobility: Users can make and receive calls from anywhere with an internet connection, using various devices such as smartphones, tablets, or laptops.
Enhanced Features: VoIP services come with advanced features like call forwarding, voicemail-to-email, video conferencing, and more, often at no additional cost.
Scalability: Easily add or remove lines as your business grows without the need for extensive hardware.
Integration with Other Services: VoIP systems can integrate with other business applications such as CRM software, enhancing overall productivity.
Improved Sound Quality: High-definition voice quality can be achieved with a good internet connection, providing clearer communication.
Reliability and Redundancy: With proper setup, VoIP systems can provide reliable communication and have redundancy options to ensure continuity of service.

What kind of internet connection is required for VoIP?
A stable broadband connection with sufficient bandwidth is required. Typically, a minimum of 100 kbps per concurrent call is recommended.
