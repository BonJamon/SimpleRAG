How to Set Up Parental Controls
Parental controls allow you to manage and restrict the type of content accessible to your children while using QuickConnect services. Follow the steps below to set up parental controls on your internet connection and devices.
Setting Up Parental Controls on Your QuickConnect Router

Log in to your router's web interface by entering 192.168.0.1 in your web browser.
Enter your username and password. The default username is admin, and the default password is admin (or check your router's manual).
Navigate to the Parental Controls section.
Enable the Parental Controls feature.
Set the rules for the devices you want to restrict by entering their MAC addresses.
Choose the categories or URLs you wish to block, and set the allowed time periods for internet access.
Click Save to apply the settings.

Setting Up Parental Controls on Individual Devices
Windows

Open Settings and navigate to Accounts.
Select Family & other users and click on Add a family member.
Add a child account and follow the prompts.
Once the child account is created, log in with that account and go to Family Safety.
Set the restrictions such as app limits, screen time, and content filters.

macOS

Open System Preferences and click on Screen Time.
Enable Screen Time and select your child's account from the options.
Set the desired restrictions under App Limits, Downtime, and Content & Privacy settings.
Click OK to save the changes.

Setting Up Parental Controls on Streaming Services
Netflix

Log in to your Netflix account and navigate to Account Settings.
Select Parental Controls.
Enter your Netflix password and create a 4-digit PIN.
Select the maturity level for the content and chosen profiles.
Click Save to apply the parental controls.
