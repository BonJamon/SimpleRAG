QuickConnect Customer Complaint Processing Guide

Steps for Processing a Customer Complaint

  1. Listen to the customer's complaint carefully and ensure you understand the issue.
  2. Acknowledge the complaint and express empathy towards the customer's situation.
  3. Gather all necessary information from the customer, including:
    • Customer's full name
    • Account number
    • Contact information
    • Details of the complaint
  4. Document the complaint in the customer management system.
  5. Provide the customer with a timeline for resolution and any next steps.
  6. Follow up with the appropriate department to address the complaint.
  7. Keep the customer informed of any updates or resolutions.
  8. Ensure the issue is fully resolved before marking the complaint as closed.

Information to Collect from the Customer

Information Type Description
Customer's Full Name Name of the customer lodging the complaint.
Account Number Unique number assigned to the customer's account.
Contact Information Customer's phone number and/or email address.
Details of the Complaint Specific details regarding the customer's issue or concern.

Example Complaint Dialogue

Below is an example of how to handle a customer complaint over the phone:

Customer: "I'm having trouble with my internet connection. It's been down for the past two days."

Agent: "I'm very sorry to hear about your internet issues. Let me take some information to help resolve this for you."

  • "May I have your full name and account number?"
  • "Could you please provide your best contact number and email address?"
  • "Can you describe what's been happening with your internet connection?"

Agent: "Thank you for providing that information. I will log this complaint and our technical team will look into this issue. You should hear back from us within the next 24 hours with an update."

Note: Always assure the customer that their complaint is being taken seriously and provide realistic timelines for follow-up and resolution.

Helpful Practices

  • Stay calm and patient throughout the conversation.
  • Show empathy and understanding.
  • Be clear and concise in your communication.
  • Record all details accurately.
  • Follow up until the complaint is fully resolved.
