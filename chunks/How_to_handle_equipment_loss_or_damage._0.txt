QuickConnect Customer Service Guide - Handling Equipment Loss or Damage
Handling Equipment Loss or Damage
Note: Please ensure all interactions are logged in the system and follow the company's policies strictly.
Steps to Handle Equipment Loss
Verify Customer Information: Ensure that you have the correct customer details including account number, full name, and contact information.
Identify Lost Equipment: Ask the customer to specify the equipment that is lost. Common items include routers, modems, and set-top boxes.
Check Warranty and Coverage: Check if the equipment is under warranty or covered by any insurance plans offered by QuickConnect.
Provide Replacement Options: Inform the customer about replacement options and associated costs, if not covered.
Arrange for Replacement: Organize the dispatch of replacement equipment. Provide the customer with a tracking number if applicable.
Update Records: Log the incident in the system and update the customer's account with relevant details.
Steps to Handle Equipment Damage
Verify Customer Information: Verify the customer's account details, similar to the loss procedure.
Assess Damage: Ask the customer to describe the damage or provide photos if possible. This helps in assessing whether the equipment is repairable or needs replacement.
Check Warranty and Coverage: Determine if the damaged equipment is under warranty or if damage protection plans apply.
Provide Resolution Options: Discuss repair or replacement based on warranty and coverage. Inform the customer of any costs involved if it is not covered.
Organize Repairs/Replacement: Arrange for repair services or dispatch a replacement device. Provide relevant instructions for returning damaged equipment if necessary.
Follow-Up: Schedule a follow-up to ensure the customer has received the replacement/repair and is satisfied with the resolution.
Update Records: Log all details and updates related to the damage report in the customer's account records.

Warranty Coverage
Standard equipment warranties cover manufacturing defects but not lost or customer-caused damage. Verify coverage before proceeding.
Insurance Plans
Customers may opt for equipment insurance. Check if the customer has an active plan that covers loss or damage.
Customer Responsibility
Customers are usually responsible for lost or damaged equipment unless covered by insurance or an extended warranty.
