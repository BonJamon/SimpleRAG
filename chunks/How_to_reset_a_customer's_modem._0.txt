QuickConnect - Resetting a Customer's Modem
How to Reset a Customer's Modem
Resetting a modem can solve many connectivity issues for customers. Follow the steps below to guide a customer through the process.
Steps to Reset a Modem
Power Cycle the Modem
Ask the customer to unplug the modem's power cord from the electrical outlet.
Wait for at least 30 seconds.
Plug the power cord back into the outlet.
Wait for the modem to fully restart (this can take up to 2 minutes).
Check Lights on the Modem
Ensure that the lights on the modem are blinking in the proper sequence. Refer to the modem's manual for the correct light sequence.
If there are any red or blinking error lights, proceed to a hard reset.
Hard Reset the Modem (Factory Reset)
Locate the reset button on the modem. It is usually a small, recessed button.
Using a paperclip or pin, press and hold the reset button for 15-20 seconds.
Release the reset button and wait for the modem to restart fully.
Common Modem Issues and Solutions
Issue
Possible Solution
No Internet Connection
Check the physical connections, ensure the modem's lights are properly lit, and perform a power cycle.
Slow Internet Speed
Reset the modem, check for any ongoing outages in the area, and make sure no other devices are consuming excessive bandwidth.
Intermittent Internet Connection
Perform a hard reset of the modem, check for interference from other electronic devices, and verify the quality of the signal strength.
Modem Not Powering On
Ensure the power cord is securely connected, try a different electrical outlet, and check for any visible damage to the power cord.
Contact Technical Support
If the customer continues to experience issues after following these steps, advise them to contact QuickConnect's Technical Support for further assistance.
