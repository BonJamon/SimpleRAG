Understanding Roaming Charges
Roaming charges are fees incurred when a customer uses their mobile phone service outside their home network's coverage area. Below is detailed information to help you support customers with their questions about roaming charges.
Common Scenarios
Scenario
Description
Charges
International Roaming
Using mobile services while traveling abroad.
Can be significantly higher than domestic rates.
Domestic Roaming
Using mobile services outside the customer's home area but within the same country.
Usually minimal or no extra charges, depending on the plan.
Data Roaming
Internet usage (browsing, streaming, etc.) outside the home network.
Data charges can be very high; some plans offer special data packages.
Voice Roaming
Making or receiving calls outside the home network.
Additional per-minute charges often apply.
SMS Roaming
Sending or receiving text messages outside the home network.
May incur additional charges per message.
Tips for Reducing Roaming Charges
Advise customers to purchase an international plan before traveling.
Encourage the use of Wi-Fi for data services whenever possible.
Suggest turning off data roaming in their phone settings.
Inform about the availability of local SIM cards or travel SIM packs.
Check if their current plan offers any special roaming packages.
FAQs
Q: How can customers check their roaming charges?
A: Customers can log into their QuickConnect account online or use the QuickConnect mobile app to check their roaming charges.
