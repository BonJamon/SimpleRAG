How to Customize Wi-Fi Network Names

Follow the steps below to customize the Wi-Fi network name (SSID) for your QuickConnect router:

Step Details
1 Open a web browser.
Use any web browser (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Safari).
2 Enter the router's IP address.
Type 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1 into the address bar and press Enter.
Note: If neither IP address works, check the router's manual or the label on the router.
3 Log in to the router's admin panel.
Enter the username and password. The default is often admin/admin or admin/password.
Note: If you've changed the credentials and can't remember them, you might need to reset your router.
4 Navigate to the Wireless Settings.
Look for a section named "Wireless," "Wi-Fi," or something similar.
5 Locate the SSID field.
Find the field labeled "SSID" (this is your network name).
6 Enter your desired network name.
Type in your preferred Wi-Fi network name.
7 Save the changes.
Click on the "Save" or "Apply" button to save your new settings.
8 Restart your router (if necessary).
Some routers may require a reboot for the changes to take effect.

Additional Tips

  • Use a unique, easy-to-remember name for your Wi-Fi network to avoid confusion with nearby networks.
  • Avoid including sensitive information in your network name (e.g., your address or full name).
  • If using a dual-band router, consider setting different names for the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz networks for easier identification.
