Troubleshooting Video Call Quality

Introduction

Poor video call quality can be frustrating for our customers. This document provides a step-by-step guide for customer service agents to troubleshoot video call quality issues effectively. Follow these guidelines to ensure our customers have a satisfactory experience.

Common Issues and Solutions

Issue Possible Cause Solution
Choppy or Laggy Video
  • Insufficient bandwidth
  • Background applications consuming bandwidth
  • Check network speed.
  • Close unnecessary applications.
  • Upgrade network plan if necessary.
Frozen Video
  • Network congestion
  • Outdated software
  • Restart router.
  • Update video calling software.
Pixelated Video
  • Low-resolution settings
  • Weak signal strength
  • Adjust video resolution settings.
  • Improve Wi-Fi signal strength.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting Guide

  1. Check Internet Connection:
    • Ask the customer to run a speed test and share the results.
    • Advise the customer to restart their router/modem.
  2. Close Background Applications:
    • Guide the customer to close any unnecessary applications that may be using bandwidth.
  3. Update Software:
    • Ensure the customer’s video calling software and system drivers are up to date.
  4. Adjust Video Resolution:
    • Recommend lowering the video resolution in the video calling application's settings if poor quality persists.
  5. Check Device Performance:
    • Ask if the customer’s device meets the minimum system requirements for the video calling software.
